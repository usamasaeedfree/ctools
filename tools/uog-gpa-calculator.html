How to Calculate GPA in UOG?

Calculating your GPA at the University of Gujrat (UOG) is simple! The basic formula is to divide your total Grade Points by your total Credit Hours.

Step-by-step method:
  1. Find your Grade Points: For each subject, multiply your grade point (e.g., A=4.0, B+=3.3, B=3.0) by the credit hours of that subject.
  2. Add them up: Sum all the grade points you earned from all your subjects.
  3. Divide: Divide the total grade points by the total credit hours of all your subjects. The result is your GPA!
Example:
  • Subject 1: Grade B (3.0 points) with 3 credit hours. Grade Points = 3.0 * 3 = 9.0
  • Subject 2: Grade A (4.0 points) with 2 credit hours. Grade Points = 4.0 * 2 = 8.0
  • Total Grade Points = 9.0 + 8.0 = 17.0
  • Total Credit Hours = 3 + 2=5
  • Your GPA = 17.0 ÷ 5=3.4

Our UOG GPA calculator does this for you automatically, so you don't have to do it by hand!

How much CGPA is 3.5 GPA?

GPA is for one semester, while CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is your overall GPA for all semesters combined.

If you have a GPA of 3.5 for a semester, and you want to see what your overall CGPA would look like, you would add this GPA to your previous semesters' GPAs and divide by the number of semesters.

For example, if your previous CGPA was 3.3 and you got a 3.5 in the current semester, your new CGPA would be somewhere between 3.3 and 3.5.

To get an exact CGPA: You need to calculate it using the formula:

CGPA = (Total Grade Points from all semesters) ÷ (Total Credit Hours from all semesters).


What is 84% in CGPA?

Just like with GPA, converting a percentage like 84% into CGPA depends on the specific grading scale used by the university. However, in many Pakistani universities, a simple conversion can be made.

An 84% score is generally considered a high grade. On a 4.0 scale, it would likely fall into the A- or B+ category, which could be around 3.7 or 3.3 CGPA points.

A common formula used is:
  • CGPA = (Your Percentage) ÷ 9.5

So, using this general formula:

  • CGPA = 84 ÷ 9.5 ≈ 8.84 (on a 10-point scale)

If you are on a 4.0 scale, the conversion might be different. The best way to know for sure is to use the official UOG grading scale.
