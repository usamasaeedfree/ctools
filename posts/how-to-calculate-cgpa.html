What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. This is the average of your GPA across all semesters.

In simple terms:
  • GPA = Your result/performance in one semester
  • CGPA = Your average result of all semesters combined

If you've completed 4 semesters, CGPA will be the weighted average of your GPA across all those 4 semesters.


How to Calculate CGPA — Step-by-Step Guide

The CGPA calculation formula is easy, and I personally use this method after every semester. You just need two things:

  1. Credit hours of each subject
  2. Grade points (based on your subject grades)

Let me explain the full process.


Step 1: Know Your Grade Points

Most universities in Pakistan follow a 4.0 GPA scale system.

Grade Grade Point
A 4.0
A- 3.7
B+ 3.3
B 3.0
C+ 2.7
C 2.3
D 1.0
F 0.0

Step 2: Note Subject Credit Hours

Every subject has its own credit hour weight:

  • Theory subject = usually 3 credit hours
  • Lab course = usually 1 credit hour

Step 3: Multiply Grade Point X Credit Hours

Now for each subject, multiply the grade point by its credit hour to find the quality points.

Example:
Subject CH Grade GP CH * GP
Math 3 A 4.0 12.0
Physics 3 B 3.0 9.0
Computer Lab 1 A- 3.7 3.7

Total CH = 7 | Total Quality Points = 24.7


Step 4: Use the CGPA Formula

CGPA = Total Quality Points / tootal Credit Hours Example: CGPA = 24.7 / 7 = 3.53

Your CGPA is 3.53 out of 4.0


How to Calculate CGPA After Multiple Semesters

If you've completed more than one semester, here's how to find the overall CGPA:

Formula:

Overall CGPA = (GPA of each semester * Credit Hours of semester) / Total Credit Hours

Example:

Semester GPA CH
1st 3.5 17
2nd 3.2 18
3rd 3.8 15

Now multiply GPA * CH:

  • 3.5 * 17 = 59.5
  • 3.2 * 18 = 57.6
  • 3.8 * 15 = 57

Total Quality Points = 174.1
Total CH = 50

CGPA = 174.1 / 50 = 3.48

Common Mistakes Students Make

These are some mistakes I made personally and also noticed among friends:

  • Using Wrong Grade Points: Every university may use slightly different grading rules. Always confirm your grade scale.
  • Forgetting Credit Hours: Never ignore CH — it's essential for accuracy.
  • Using Round Numbers:Always include decimals in GPA; rounding can reduce accuracy.
  • Ignoring Labs: Lab subjects have credit hours too; they also affect CGPA.

Avoid these, and you'll always get a correct result.


FAQs — Students' Common Questions

Is CGPA the same as Percentage?

No. CGPA is a grade scale (like 3.5/4.0), while percentage is a numeric score (like 87%). Use conversion formulas if needed.

Is CGPA calculated the same way in all universities?

Mostly yes, but some universities have slight variations. The basic formula remains the same.

What if I fail in one subject?

If you get an F (Grade Point = 0), it will drastically lower your CGPA. You should retake the course to improve your average.
